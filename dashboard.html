Sports Injury Resource Hub

Clinical guides, diagnostic details, and structured recovery pathways for common athletic conditions.

Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL) Tear

Knee Joint
Clinical Overview

A complete rupture or partial tear of the ACL, usually resulting from rapid cutting, pivoting, or landing deceleration forces common in soccer, basketball, and skiing.

Key Diagnostic Indicator

Lachman Test: Increased anterior tibial translation with a soft or mushy end-point, indicating loss of anterior translation constraint.

Standard Recovery Timeline

6 to 12 months. Reconstruction surgery (autograft) is typically followed by a structured 5-phase progressive sports physical therapy program.

Rotator Cuff Tendon Tear

Shoulder Joint
Clinical Overview

Tear of the supraspinatus, infraspinatus, or subscapularis tendon, caused by acute trauma (falls) or chronic overhead micro-trauma from throwing, swimming, or tennis.

Key Diagnostic Indicator

Empty Can (Jobe) Test: Weakness or pain during abduction to 90° and forward flexion to 30° with internal rotation, pointing to supraspinatus pathology.

Standard Recovery Timeline

4 to 6 months. Arthroscopic suture anchoring and decompression requires passive immobilization in a sling before active strength loading begins.

Meniscus Tear (Medial or Lateral)

Knee Joint
Clinical Overview

Damage to the fibrocartilage pads of the knee joint. Medial meniscus tears are more frequent. Often occurs during twisting loads when the knee is loaded in flexion.

Key Diagnostic Indicator

McMurray Test: A palpable or audible click/snap during passive flexion and internal/external tibial rotation under compressive loads.

Standard Recovery Timeline

Partial Meniscectomy: 4-6 weeks (rapid recovery). Meniscus Repair: 3-5 months (slower due to protected healing of vascular zones).

Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome (PFPS)

Knee Joint
Clinical Overview

Often called "Runner's Knee." Characterized by anterior knee pain around or behind the patella, driven by poor tracking of the patella within the femoral groove.

Key Diagnostic Indicator

Patellar Compression Test: Elicits pain when compressing the patella while asking the patient to contract the quadriceps.

Standard Recovery Timeline

6 to 12 weeks. Conservative physical therapy focusing on vastus medialis obliquus (VMO) strengthening, hip abduction strength, and ankle mobility adjustments.

Lumbar Herniated Nucleus Pulposus (HNP)

Spinal Column
Clinical Overview

Displacement of core disc material beyond the annulus boundary, compressing spinal nerve roots (commonly L4-L5 or L5-S1) and causing radiculopathy (sciatic pain).

Key Diagnostic Indicator

Straight Leg Raise (SLR) Test: Positive if raising the leg between 30° to 70° reproduces radiating sciatic pain down the posterior leg.

Standard Recovery Timeline

8 to 16 weeks (Conservative) or 3 to 5 months (Surgical Microdiscectomy). Focuses on neutral spine stability and eliminating compressive loads.
